Cyborgs and cybernetics

From what I understand, Donna Haraway published The Cyborg Manifesto at a time when academia was debating identity politics, particularly within feminist/intellectual circles. Where theorists were considering intersectionality, Haraway came to an opposing conclusion, one that stirred a lot of thoughts (but in recent years has been somewhat discredited, including by Haraway herself).

What follows is a summary of my understanding.

The Cyborg Manifesto rejects the binary oppositions that dominate our lives. Specifically, the manifesto discusses the modern binaries of:

  • human/animal
  • animal-human/machine
  • physical/non-physical
These binaries are produced and constructed in a Western (read: patriarchal, heteronormative, capitalist, etc) point-of-view.

Here's where cyborg theory comes into it.

Cybernetics is the study of systems of control, in this case, anthropological control. You can consider capitalism, patriarchy, family systems, and more as a cybernetic system. In these cybernetic systems, you have cyborgs, the subjects of the system and controls.
